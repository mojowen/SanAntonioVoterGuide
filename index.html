How to Vote

What You’ve Gotta Bring

All voters in Texas are now required to bring a valid form of photo identification to vote.

Acceptable forms of identification (you only need to bring 1 of the following):

  • Texas driver license issued by the Texas Department of Public Safety (DPS)
  • Texas Election Identification Certificate issued by DPS
  • Texas personal identification card issued by DPS
  • Texas concealed handgun license issued by DPS
  • United States military identification card containing the person’s photograph
  • United States citizenship certificate containing the person’s photograph
  • United States passport

Bringing a voter registration certificate (proof of registration) is not required but is highly recommended.

Where You’ve Gotta Go

To find out when the polls will be open or what your Election Day polling site is, visit the Bexar County Elections Department.

Ballot Measures

Proposition 1

What it does

Edwards Aquifer Protection Renewal

A yes Vote Means...

San Antonio can continue to protect the aquifer and land above it, securing our primary water supply

endorse

A no Vote Means...

The program to protect and improve the Edwards Aquifer will lose its funding.

endorse

Recommended: yes

Proposition 2

What it does

Linear Trails Program

A yes Vote Means...

San Antonio can expand its system of trails and parks around the city

endorse

A no Vote Means...

San Antonio's parks will lose a major source of funding.

endorse

Recommended: yes

Charter Amendment 1

What it does

Public Vote for Railway Projects

A yes Vote Means...

A public vote will be mandatory for any railway project that the City wants to push forward.

endorse

A no Vote Means...

City Council can decide to approve or fail railway projects in San Antonio

endorse

Recommended: yes

Charter Amendment 2

What it does

City Council Pay

A yes Vote Means...

Effective July 1st, City Councilors will be paid $45,722 per year and the Mayor will be paid $61,725.

endorse

A no Vote Means...

City Councilor and Mayoral pay will stay the same: Councilors will earn $1,040 per year and the Mayor will earn $4,040 per year.

endorse

Recommended: yes
